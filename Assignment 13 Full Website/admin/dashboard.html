Sad News

It is with heavy hearts that the Gunsmithing Club of America family announces the passing of Master Gunsmith, AGI Instructor, GunTech Editor and longtime friend, Jack Landis from an extended illness.

Jack has been with AGI for over 16 years and helped found the Gunsmithing Club 15 years ago. Jack was instrumental in building our foundation, championing our evolution and guiding our sustainability over all these years. His humor, observational wit, and extensive knowledge, helped make GunTech what it is. He was also a patient, helpful mentor to many AGI students who benefited from his experience and flourished with his guidance.

I personally spent countless hours with him; on the range testing firearms for GunTech, behind the scenes working & planning course development, over dinners strategizing how to build the organization that he loved, over drinks laughing after exhausting hours working various trade shows, and through hundreds of phone calls over the years organizing the work required to accomplish the missions of the American Gunsmithing Institute and the Gunsmithing Club of America. Jack worked tirelessly to execute on our vision, making huge contributions and now leaving big shoes to fill.

Jack was also a friend who I admired for his broad knowledge of firearms and Gunsmithing, among many other subjects. (He also jokingly claimed to know the “Secrets of the Universe and How Women think”). He also corrected my grammar and punctuation to make me look good, which was almost a full time job in and of itself!
